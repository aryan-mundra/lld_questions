♟️ Chess Game — Low Level Design

Learn by building it the way an interviewer expects: understand → gather requirements → design one requirement at a time → integrate → dry run.

1 · Understand the problem

Before any code, say the problem out loud in plain English. Build intuition first.

📝 Problem statement

Design the software for a two-player chess game. There's an 8×8 board of pieces. Players alternate turns; each piece type moves by its own rule. A move must be legal, and capturing lands your piece where an enemy piece stood. The game ends with a winner. (We'll do a basic version: win by capturing the king — real check/checkmate is noted as an extension.)

ACTORS Who uses it?

  • Two players — White and Black, moving in turn.
  • System — validates moves, applies captures, tracks status.

ENTITIES What "things" exist?

  • Board, Cell (square)
  • Piece (King, Queen, Rook, Bishop, Knight, Pawn)
  • Player, Move, Game

BEHAVIOR What happens? (the flow)

player picks from→to → is it legal for THAT piece? → apply move (+ capture) → other player's turn

The star of this problem: each piece has a different movement rule. Handle that with polymorphism (each piece answers "can I move here?") — not a giant switch.

🧩 Design pattern in focus

Polymorphism (Open/Closed Principle) — each piece type owns its own canMove() rule, so the game never switches on piece type and new pieces plug in as new subclasses. You'll see it in requirement F2 and formalize it in NF1.

Compare: this is plain inheritance-based polymorphism — not State (vending machine) or Strategy (elevator/parking).

🔗 Relationships (in plain words)

──▷
IS-A: King / Queen / Rook / Bishop / Knight / Pawn is a Piece (each overrides canMove).
◆──
HAS: Board owns an 8×8 of Cells; each Cell may hold a Piece; ChessGame owns the Board, players and move history.
- -▷
USES: ChessGame asks the Piece canMove(); sliding pieces ask the Board isPathClear().

🌊 End-to-end flow

makeMove(White, e2 → e4)
  └─ your turn? your piece? not onto own piece?
  └─ piece.canMove(board, start, end)? ← the polymorphic call
  └─ apply: capture (if any) + move piece + record Move
  └─ captured a King? → status = WHITE_WON, else switch turn ♟️
